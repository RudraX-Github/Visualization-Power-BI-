Madhav Ecommerce: Interactive Analysis

📊 Executive Summary

This report details the analysis of the Madhav Ecommerce dataset, visualized in the accompanying Power BI dashboard.

The analysis reveals strong revenue and AOV, driven primarily by clothing and key customers in Maharashtra. However, it also identified significant underperformance in Delhi and Gujarat, heavy reliance on COD payments, and seasonal profit volatility.

⚠️ Problem Statement

Despite strong overall sales, this analysis identifies key areas for improvement. The business faces regional imbalances, product concentration, and seasonal profit dips that present risks to sustainable growth.

Key Challenges Identified:

  • Underperformance in high-potential states like Delhi and Gujarat.
  • Heavy reliance on clothing as the primary product category.
  • Cash on Delivery (COD) dominating payment methods, which increases operational risk and costs.
  • Significant profit dips in months like April and June, indicating poor seasonal planning.

💡 Proposed Solutions

1. Regional Sales Optimization

  1. Launch localized, data-driven marketing campaigns in Delhi and Gujarat.
  2. Offer region-specific discounts or bundles to attract new customers.
  3. Partner with local influencers to build trust and improve market penetration.

2. Product Diversification

  1. Expand and promote profitable sub-categories: printers, bookcases, and storage.
  2. Introduce seasonal collections to test demand and create urgency.
  3. Implement a cross-selling strategy based on customer purchase history.

3. Payment Strategy Enhancement

  1. Aggressively incentivize UPI and digital payments with small cashback or loyalty points.
  2. Gradually reduce COD availability for high-return categories to cut logistical overhead.

4. Seasonal Profit Planning

  1. Run targeted promotions (e.g., "Summer Sale") in April and June to boost off-season engagement.
  2. Realign inventory and ad spend to capitalize on peak profit months like January and March.
